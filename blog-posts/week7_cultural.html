My Values

Matthew D. Huff

October 13, 2014

Today, I'm being asked to explore my values. Hm. Yeah, I can do that.

Exploring My Values

When I think about the times in my life where I've just been the most satisfied. The values of Adventure, Enthusiasm, and Self-Mastery comes to mind. When I went to Brazil at the end of my undergraduate time, I learned more about myself and had more fun than I'd had the previous 4 years. EASILY. It was kind of trippy honestly. I'd always felt like something was missing in my life, and for about a month, I had that thing. It was freaking awesome.

My Most Important Value

By a wide margin, I value Self-Mastery over just about everything. I suffered greatly due to my lack of self-mastery and discipline up to this point in my life, and I reached a point where I committed to master myself at all costs. No matter what I had to throw away or give up, I decided I would become the master of my mind and life.

I never thought I was that bad a person or anything. I was an Eagle Scout, I knew I was intelligent, but I never felt like I was in control, or had any sort of choice in how things went.

And eventually, after the incredible dissapointment that was my college experience, I realized that it was up to me to decide what kind of man I was going to be. Everything happening now is just the consequences of that paradigm shift.

As far as living up to my values is concerned. I always try to live up to my value of Self-Mastery. Everything else, I believe will come from that single idea. If I'm really master of my life, then I will find all the Enthusiasm, Adventure, Wealth, Wisdom, Etc. I could ever need.

When I think about my primary values...I get frustrated, because I don't feel like I'm living up to them yet. I also get excited because I think about a world in which I have finally managed to embody those values, and I get energized to keep going.

There really ain't much to be said. I value Self-Mastery above all things. I don't expect to be able to fulfill most other values unless I can live up to that one. That's why I meditate every day. That's why I've been slowly destroying my bad habits(As of last week, I completed a 90-day ban on weed and alcohol. Now, I've basically got no desire for either one.) and creating new good habits (Daily meditation, next up is good financial management habits)

Till next time!

Matthew D. Huff
